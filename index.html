How normalization works: Platforms measure your track's integrated loudness (LUFS) and apply gain to match their target. If your master is louder than the target, it gets turned down — no processing, just attenuation. If it's quieter, some platforms will boost it. Over-compressing to hit a specific number hurts your dynamics without any benefit.

Spotify's three modes: Quiet = −19 LUFS, Normal = −14 LUFS (default, ~87% of users), Loud = −11 LUFS. Master to sound great at Normal. If your master is louder than the selected target, Spotify applies clean gain reduction — no limiting or processing.

Why codecs matter at mastering: Lossy encoding (AAC, Ogg Vorbis, MP3) can introduce inter-sample peaks above 0 dBFS even when your master is within limits. True peak limiting to −1 dBTP or lower prevents distortion introduced during transcoding.

Note Aggressive encoding
Delivery rule of thumb: Always deliver a 24-bit WAV or FLAC at the sample rate of your session (44.1 or 48 kHz). Distributors will transcode to whatever the platform needs. Upsampling before delivery adds no quality benefit and may cause issues.
Dolby Atmos delivery standard: All platforms receiving Atmos mixes require 48 kHz / 24-bit. Using 44.1 kHz or 16-bit will fail QC. Your stereo and spatial file durations must match within 50ms.

Note on 96 kHz Atmos: While 96 kHz offers additional sonic detail and is sometimes requested for audiophile or archival releases, it doubles file size and delivery load. All streaming platforms currently require 48 kHz for distribution. Use 96 kHz only if specifically requested by a client or label for archival masters.
Podcast & dialog content uses different loudness standards than music. The AES and most podcast platforms target −16 LUFS ±1 LU for spoken word, following the same broadcast lineage as EBU R128.

Pro tip for podcast delivery: Export at −16 LUFS with −1 dBTP true peak as a 44.1 kHz / 16-bit MP3 at 128 kbps (mono) or 192 kbps (stereo). This hits the AES standard, works across all major platforms, and keeps file sizes podcast-friendly.
